Getting Started

Welcome to JPlayer! Here's how to get the most out of your media player:

  1. Launch JPlayer from your iOS home screen
  2. Grant necessary permissions for media access when prompted
  3. Browse and select your media files
  4. Enjoy seamless playback with professional controls

Frequently Asked Questions

What media formats does JPlayer support?

JPlayer supports a wide range of audio and video formats, including but not limited to MP4, MOV, M4V, MP3, AAC, and many more. The app leverages iOS's native media capabilities along with additional codec support.

How do I add media files to JPlayer?

You can add media files through multiple methods:

  • Browse files already on your device
  • Use the iOS Files app integration
  • Open media files from other apps using "Open In" functionality

Does JPlayer collect any of my data?

No. JPlayer does not collect, store, or transmit any personal data. Your privacy is our priority. All media playback and app functionality occurs locally on your device.

Can I use JPlayer offline?

Yes! JPlayer works completely offline. Once installed, you don't need an internet connection to play your local media files.

How do I control playback speed?

During playback, tap the settings icon to access playback controls where you can adjust speed, audio tracks, subtitles, and more.

Is JPlayer free to use?

Please check the App Store listing for current pricing and any available in-app purchases or premium features.

Troubleshooting

Media file won't play

  • Ensure the file format is supported
  • Check if the file is corrupted by trying to play it in another app
  • Verify you have sufficient storage space on your device
  • Try restarting the app

App crashes or freezes

  • Force quit the app and restart it
  • Ensure your iOS is updated to the latest version
  • Check if you have the latest version of JPlayer installed
  • Free up device storage if running low

Audio/Video sync issues

  • Try using the audio delay adjustment in playback settings
  • Restart playback from the beginning
  • Check if the issue exists in the original file
